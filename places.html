TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal is an Islamic religious building, mosque and tomb in India, built in the 17th century by king Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Its chief architect was Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. The building is in the city of Agra, Uttar Pradesh. It is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture (a blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles) and is one of the worldâ€™s most iconic monuments.

BADSHAHI MOSQUE

The Badshahi Mosque is one of the most iconic and historic landmarks of Lahore, Pakistan. It was built by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb in the 17th century, and is the second largest mosque in Pakistan and the fifth largest in the world. The mosque is a masterpiece of Islamic architecture, with its red sandstone exterior, marble domes, minarets, and intricate calligraphy. It can house as much as 10000 people.

FAISAL MOSQUE

The Faisal Mosque is one of the most iconic landmarks of Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan. It was designed by the renowned Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay and completed in 1986. The mosque is named after King Faisal bin Abdul Aziz of Saudi Arabia, who donated $120 million for its construction. The mosque has a unique and modern design that resembles a desert tent, with four slender minarets and a triangular prayer hall. The mosque can accommodate up to 300,000 worshippers.

EIFFEL TOWER

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world. It is a wrought-iron lattice tower that stands on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It was built between 1887 and 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair. It is named after Gustave Eiffel, the engineer who designed and constructed it. The tower is 324 meters tall and has three levels for visitors.

KHAANA KAABA

The Khana Kaaba is a sacred building in Islam, located in the city of Mecca, Saudi Arabia. It is believed to be the house of God and the direction of prayer for Muslims all over the world. The Khana Kaaba has a long history, dating back to the time of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) and his son Ismail (Ishmael), who built it according to God's command. The Khana Kaaba is a cube-shaped structure, covered with a black cloth called Kiswah, which is embroidered with gold and silver threads.

LONDON BRIDGE

London Bridge is one of the most iconic landmarks in the United Kingdom. It spans the River Thames and connects the City of London with Southwark. The bridge has a long and rich history, dating back to Roman times when the first wooden bridge was built on the site. Since then, several bridges have been constructed and destroyed by fire, war, and natural disasters. The current bridge, opened in 1973, is a modern concrete and steel structure that can withstand heavy traffic and environmental challenges.

DISNEY LAND

Disneyland is a theme park that was created by Walt Disney and opened in 1955 in Anaheim, California. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the world, attracting millions of visitors every year. Disneyland offers a variety of attractions, shows, parades, and entertainment for people of all ages and interests. Some of the iconic features of Disneyland include the Sleeping Beauty Castle, the Main Street U.S.A., the Haunted Mansion, and the Pirates of the Caribbean. Disneyland is also known for its characters, such as Mickey Mouse, Cinderella, Snow White, and many others.

TOKYO TOWER

Tokyo Tower is a famous landmark in Japan's capital city. It was built in 1958 as a communications and observation tower, inspired by the Eiffel Tower in Paris. It stands 333 meters tall and has two observation decks that offer panoramic views of Tokyo and beyond. Tokyo Tower is also a symbol of Japan's post-war recovery and economic growth. It attracts millions of visitors every year who come to admire its distinctive orange and white color and its elegant lattice structure.
