ISLAMABAD

Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan and is located at the edge of the Pothohar Plateau at the foot of the Margalla Hills in Islamabad Capital Territory. It is the country’s ninth-most populous city, with a population of over 1.2 million people. The city’s site was chosen by a commission in 1959 after Karachi was found unsuitable as the capital. The name Islamabad means City of Islam.

MAKKAH

Makkah (officially Makkah al-Mukarramah) is the holiest city in Islam and the capital of Mecca Province in the Hejaz region of western Saudi Arabia1. It is located 70 km (43 mi) inland from Jeddah on the Red Sea, in a narrow valley 277 m (909 ft) above sea level2. The city is also known as Makkah and is the official transliteration used by the Saudi government.

MADINAH

Madinah (also known as Medina) is the second-holiest city in Islam and is located in the Hejaz region of western Saudi Arabia1. It was formerly known as Yathrib2. The city is also known as Madinah An-Nabi (The City of the Prophet) or Madinah Al-Munawwarah (The Enlightened City).

NEWYORK

New York City is the most populous city in the United States and is located in southeastern New York state1. It is known for its diversity, dynamism, politics, economy, and culture2. The city is composed of five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island

PARIS

Paris is the capital city of France and is located in the north-central part of the country. It is known for its art, fashion, food, and culture. The city is home to several landmarks, including the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre-Dame Cathedral.

LONDON

London is the capital city of England and the United Kingdom and is located in southeastern England. It is known for its history, culture, and landmarks, including the Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, and the British Museum.
